What is the slope of a line?
The slope of a line is a measurement of it's steepness and it is calculated by dividing the change in y by the cahnge in x
How to calculate slope of a line?
To calculate slope of a line you should use the following formula:
m = (y2 - y2) / (x2 - x1)
where:
m = slope
(x1, y1) = coordinates of the first point in the line
(x2, y2) = coordinates of the second point in the line
Why use slope of a line?
The slope of a line is used in order to get information about the direction of the line on the coordinate plane.
